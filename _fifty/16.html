--- number: 16 layout: fifty title: Homage To James Turrell description: With contemporary art on the brain after Glows 14 and 15, I was reminded of a series of light installations I saw at Mass MOCA, created by James Turrell. One in particular, called Into the Light, was an installation in which Turrell creates a perfectly white room with no hard edges. You had to wear little booties to avoid scuffing the floors, and when you approached the walls you felt the floors curve up with a gentle slope. He sets up a series of projects which cast light about in such a way that the room itself seems to glow, color melting into color, your sense of space and direction getting confused and rewired as time passes. In this piece, I used a gradient effect to recreate the glowing, color-changing effect of Into The Light. permalink: fifty/16/ ---
